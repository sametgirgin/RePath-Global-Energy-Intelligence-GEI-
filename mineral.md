# Critical Minerals – Definitions and Key Technologies

| Mineral | Brief Technical Definition | Key Technologies That Depend on It |
|--------|-----------------------------|------------------------------------|
| **Cobalt** | A transition metal used mainly in high-energy-density battery chemistries; typically mined from copper–cobalt deposits. | Lithium-ion batteries (EVs), aerospace superalloys, energy-storage systems. |
| **Lithium** | A lightweight alkali metal essential for electrochemical energy storage; extracted from brines and hard-rock spodumene. | Lithium-ion batteries, grid storage, EVs, renewable integration. |
| **Graphite** | A crystalline carbon mineral used as the dominant anode material in batteries; occurs naturally or via synthetic production. | Battery anodes, fuel cells, nuclear moderating materials, lightweight composites. |
| **Rare Earth Elements (REEs)** | A group of 17 elements critical for magnetic, catalytic, and optical applications; typically extracted from monazite/bastnäsite ores. | Permanent magnets (wind turbines, EV motors), electronics, lasers, catalysts. |
| **Copper** | A highly conductive base metal essential for electrical systems; extracted from porphyry and sediment-hosted deposits. | Power grids, motors, EVs, renewable energy (solar, wind), electronics. |
| **Manganese** | A metal used in steelmaking and increasingly in battery cathodes; extracted from sedimentary and lateritic ores. | Steel alloys, NMC batteries, sodium-ion batteries, critical energy storage chemistries. |
| **Nickel** | A corrosion-resistant metal used in stainless steel and high-performance battery cathodes; sourced mainly from laterites and sulfides. | EV batteries (high-nickel cathodes), turbine alloys, hydrogen electrolysers. |
| **Zinc** | A metal used for galvanizing and alloying; typically mined from sulfide ores such as sphalerite. | Anti-corrosion coatings, die-cast alloys, energy storage (zinc-air & zinc-flow batteries). |
| **Platinum Group Metals (PGMs)** | Six noble metals (Pt, Pd, Rh, Ir, Os, Ru) known for catalytic and electrochemical efficiency. | Catalytic converters, hydrogen fuel cells, green hydrogen production (electrolyser catalysts). |
| **Bauxite** | The primary ore of aluminium, composed mainly of gibbsite, boehmite, and diaspore. | Aluminium production (lightweight vehicles, aerospace, transmission lines). |
| **Aluminium** | A lightweight, corrosion-resistant metal produced through electrolytic reduction of alumina. | EV bodies, aircraft frames, solar frames, power cables, packaging, heat exchangers. |
| **Tin** | A soft metal used mainly in soldering and tin plating; extracted from cassiterite ores. | Electronics solder, semiconductor packaging, autonomous systems, energy electronics. |
| **Vanadium** | A metal used in high-strength alloys and large-scale redox batteries; often recovered as a by-product from steel slag or titanomagnetite. | Vanadium redox-flow batteries, aerospace alloys, grid-scale storage. |
